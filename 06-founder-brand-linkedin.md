## The strategic role of founder brand

**Sieve evidence [P:1341]:**
- Thought leadership content: **748% ROI**

**Ahrefs evidence (Turn 9):**
- Profound: "no breakout founder persona"
- Otterly: "product-led" (no founder voice)
- Peec: "enterprise-sales-led" (no founder voice)

**The gap:** No AEO vendor has occupied the "category educator / thought leader" slot. First founder to build this position wins category authority within 12 months.

### Target positioning
**"The measurement expert for AI visibility."**

Think: Lenny Rachitsky is to product management what AnswerMonk's founder should be to AI visibility.

## The content strategy

### Pillar mix (4 content pillars)

| Pillar | % of posts | Purpose | Example topics |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. AEO Education** | 40% | Category authority + SEO halo | "How AI engines choose citations", "AEO vs SEO differences", "Why your brand isn't cited in ChatGPT" |
| **2. Building in Public** | 30% | Transparency + founder narrative | "Week 4 of building AnswerMonk", "What our first 50 customers taught us", "Cutting our AI chat feature" |
| **3. Industry POV / Hot Takes** | 20% | Differentiation + shareability | "Profound's $96M raise signals X", "LLMs will fragment search within 2 years", "Why AEO is bigger than SEO" |
| **4. Personal / Behind-the-Scenes** | 10% | Human connection + retention | "My mom asked what I do...", "The rejection email that changed everything", "5 years of travel taught me..." |

### Posting cadence

**Minimum:** 3 posts/week (Tue/Thu/Fri)
**Target:** 5 posts/week (Mon/Tue/Wed/Thu/Fri)
**Best time:** 8-10am PT / 11am-1pm ET (highest B2B engagement)

### Post format mix

| Format | % of posts | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Text posts (500-1,200 chars) | 50% | Quick insights, observations |
| Carousels (5-10 slides) | 25% | Frameworks, step-by-step, data |
| Short videos (30-90 sec) | 10% | Founder-faced content, Demos |
| Images + text | 10% | Product screenshots, charts |
| Polls | 5% | Engagement + audience research |

## Engagement tactics

### Response habits
- Respond to EVERY comment within 2 hours (during business hours)
- Like every comment (signals engagement)
- Follow-up with substantive replies, not just "thanks"
- Tag relevant people in response comments

### Reciprocity
- Comment thoughtfully on 10 other posts/day in your network
- Focus on content from: target ICP, potential partners, B2B marketing influencers
- Never comment-spam — only where you add genuine value

### Network building
- Connection requests: 20-30/day to target ICP personas
- Message (not spam) new connections with value: "Saw you shared X — here's related data from our research"

### LinkedIn engagement groups
- DO NOT use engagement pods (algorithm detects and suppresses)
- DO build genuine relationships with 20-30 creators in the space
- Reciprocal support (natural, not transactional)

## Content creation workflow

### Weekly ritual (2 hours, Sunday evening)

**Step 1 (30 min): Review metrics from past week**
- Which post got most engagement? Why?
- Which post tanked? Why?
- What questions did people ask? (Content ideas)

**Step 2 (45 min): Draft next week's posts**
- Pick format mix per week plan
- Draft all 3-5 posts (can be rough)
- Hook + body + CTA for each

**Step 3 (30 min): Schedule via Buffer/Typefully**
- Set exact publish times
- Queue in order
- Add images/carousels
- Final proofread

**Step 4 (15 min): Plan engagement**
- Identify 10 posts to comment on next week
- DM 5 target accounts

### Daily habit (20-30 min during week)
- 10 min: Respond to yesterday's comments
- 10 min: Comment on 5-10 posts in network
- 5-10 min: Reply to DMs
- Quick post if breaking news or insight

## LinkedIn content measurement

### Primary metrics
- Followers (weekly growth)
- Post impressions
- Engagement rate (avg engagement / impressions)
- Comments (most valuable signal)
- Shares (amplification)
- Inbound DMs (conversion signal)

### Success benchmarks

| Metric | Month 1 | Month 3 | Month 6 | Month 12 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Followers | 500-1K | 2-5K | 5-10K | 15-25K |
| Weekly impressions | 5-10K | 25-50K | 100K+ | 250K+ |
| Engagement rate | 3-5% | 5-8% | 8-12% | 10-15% |
| Inbound DMs/week | 2-5 | 10-20 | 30-50 | 50-100 |
| Leads from LinkedIn | 2-5 | 20-40 | 50-100 | 100-200 |

### Kill criteria
- Week 4: <500 followers → review content quality, posting frequency, format
- Week 8: <2% engagement rate → content feels inauthentic; adjust
- Week 12: <5 inbound DMs/week → founder brand isn't converting; diagnose

## Anti-patterns (kill these)

1. ❌ "Just launched!" posts without context/data
2. ❌ Tagging 20+ people in a post (algorithm punishes)
3. ❌ Cross-posting identical content from Twitter (LinkedIn has different norms)
4. ❌ Engagement pods (LinkedIn detects and suppresses)
5. ❌ Generic "Happy Monday!" posts (scroll past)
6. ❌ Corporate-speak ("We are excited to announce...")
7. ❌ Too many hashtags (3-5 max)
8. ❌ Links in the body of the post (reduces reach 30-40%)
9. ❌ Pitching in DMs without context ("Hi, want to buy my software?")
10. ❌ Missing a week without explanation (momentum dies fast)

## The non-negotiable

**If the founder doesn't commit to 3-5 LinkedIn posts/week for 6 months, this plan's distribution leg is 40% weaker.**

If founder can't or won't:
- Hire a content partner (ghost-writer or agency specializing in founder brand)
- Minimum viable: 2 posts/week personally written + 1 supported/edited

**Founder-led = irreplaceable.** Ghost-written should only augment, never replace.
