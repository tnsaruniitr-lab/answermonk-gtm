# 01 — ICP, Positioning, and Pricing

**Purpose:** Lock the fundamentals. Who AnswerMonk sells to, what we sell them, and what it costs. Every content, channel, and messaging decision flows from these answers.

---

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Primary ICP

**B2B SaaS Marketing Leaders** at companies with $5M – $500M ARR

**Target personas:**
- CMO / VP Marketing (buyer + economic decision maker)
- Head of Demand Generation (pipeline owner)
- Head of Content / SEO (practitioner user)
- Head of Growth (for PLG companies)

**Company stage:** Series B through Series D (enough ARR to have dedicated marketing, not so large they've built internal tooling)

**Why this slice:**
- Marketing-aware (understand SEO → AEO is obvious next step)
- Budget authority ($500-5,000/mo marketing tool purchases)
- Feel the pain directly (their category queries moving to ChatGPT daily)
- Early adopter profile (willing to try category-defining tools)
- LinkedIn-native audience (reach through founder brand)

### Secondary ICP (expand to month 3+)

**B2B SaaS marketing agencies** serving B2B SaaS clients
- White-label opportunity
- Multi-brand tracking need
- Authority in the vertical they serve

**B2B SaaS series A startups** ($1M-5M ARR)
- Not primary because budget is tighter
- But high intent for AEO given category is nascent
- Can convert via lower tier (Starter)

### Wedge sub-segment

Within the Primary ICP, the sharpest wedge is:

**Marketing-tech-adjacent SaaS** (CRM, email tools, analytics, sales tools, content platforms)

Why this subset:
- They're most AEO-aware (their prospects ask AI for tool recommendations)
- They sell to marketers (same audience as AnswerMonk)
- They're competitive (willing to invest to gain edge)
- They have content teams (can act on AnswerMonk's recommendations)

Example companies in the wedge:
- Clearscope, Semrush, Ahrefs (SEO tools — already know AEO matters)
- HubSpot, Marketo, ActiveCampaign (marketing platforms)
- Apollo, Outreach, Salesloft (sales tools)
- Amplitude, Mixpanel, Heap (analytics)
- Webflow, Contentful, Strapi (CMS)

### Deprioritize (don't target pre-launch)

- **Consumer apps** — different buying behavior, fewer enterprise deals
- **Enterprise Fortune 500** — 9-18 month sales cycles, wrong timing
- **D2C e-commerce** — different positioning needed (product discovery, not brand)
- **Agencies under 20 employees** — price-sensitive, high churn
- **Government / non-profit** — procurement complexity, compliance overhead

## Positioning

### The positioning statement (lock this exactly)

**"AnswerMonk tracks your brand's visibility across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Mode — and tells you exactly what to do to get cited more."**

### Why this positioning works

1. **Concrete platforms named** — "AI" is abstract; naming the 4 engines is specific
2. **"Tracks" + "tells you what to do"** — measurement + action, not just one
3. **"Get cited more"** — concrete outcome, not vague "visibility improvement"
4. **B2B-appropriate tone** — professional, not consumer-hype

### Positioning by audience (derivative messaging)

| Audience | Positioning variant |
|---|---|
| **CMO (buyer)** | "Know if your brand is being cited by AI — or being replaced by competitors" |
| **Head of Demand** | "The AEO equivalent of your Google Search Console" |
| **Head of Content** | "Stop guessing what content gets cited by ChatGPT. See it." |
| **Head of Growth (PLG)** | "Your brand's AI visibility score, tracked weekly across 4 engines" |
| **Investor audience** | "The measurement layer for the AEO category" |
| **Press** | "Category-defining analytics for the AI search revolution" |

### Category naming

**Primary category term:** "AI Visibility Platform"

**Why not "AEO tool" or "GEO tool":**
- "AEO/GEO" terminology is contested (peec.ai vs Profound disagree publicly)
- "AI visibility" is concrete + buyer-friendly
- Searchable + category-defining (not yet owned)

**Use "AEO" as a technique, not a category name:**
- ✅ "Tools that power Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)"
- ✅ "AEO is the practice of optimizing for AI citations"
- ❌ "The AEO Platform" (category is unsettled — risky to brand)

### Elevator pitch variants

**10-second version:**
"AnswerMonk shows you where your brand appears in AI answers across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google AI Mode."

**30-second version:**
"You know how Google Search Console tells you where you rank on Google? AnswerMonk is the equivalent for AI search engines. We track your brand's citations across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google AI Mode — and tell you exactly what's working, what's broken, and what to fix. Think of it as your AI visibility dashboard."

**2-minute version (for podcasts / press):**
"Search is fragmenting. Five years ago, if someone wanted to find you, they Googled. Now half the time they ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, or Gemini. And those AI engines don't rank you like Google does — they either cite your brand in their answer, or they don't. There's no 'page 2.' You're either in the answer or invisible. The problem is: no one can see what AI is saying about their brand. Your SEO tools don't track it. Your analytics can't see it. AnswerMonk is the first platform built specifically to measure, diagnose, and improve your brand's visibility across the 4 major AI engines. We track citations, benchmark against competitors, and tell you exactly what to do to get cited more. Our customers typically see a 30-50% improvement in AI citation rate within 90 days."

### Free tier strategy (Sieve tension resolved)

Sieve [PB:437] warns against permanent free tiers. AnswerMonk uses **free TOOLS, not free PRODUCT:**

- **Free tools:** Single-check audits, no account required, email-gated results
- **Paid product:** Continuous monitoring, dashboard, alerts, multi-brand — $149 minimum from day 1

Example:
- FREE: "Check your AI visibility score" (single-check tool, shows current state)
- PAID: "Track your AI visibility continuously" (full product, $149/mo)

Distinction is clear — free demonstrates value, paid delivers it.

### Pricing rationale

| Decision | Reason |

| Starter at $149 | Above $99 "hobby tool" perception, below $199 "committed tool" perception. Matches Pro tier of most SEO tools. |

## Competitive positioning

### Vs Profound
**Their strength:** Category leader, $96M funded, 27K traffic
**Their weakness:** Enterprise-focused, complex UX, expensive
**AnswerMonk's wedge:** "Same category insights at 1/5 the price. Built for marketing teams, not enterprise buyers."

### Vs Otterly.ai
**Their strength:** Early entrant, brand recognition in AEO community
**Their weakness:** Losing traction (79% traffic drop), thin content, product limitations
**AnswerMonk's wedge:** "The modern alternative to Otterly — built for 2026, not 2024."

### Vs Peec AI
**Their strength:** Technical depth, raised $21M Series A
**Their weakness:** Enterprise sales motion, slow product velocity
**AnswerMonk's wedge:** "Self-serve AI visibility tracking. Sign up, see your score in 2 minutes."

### Vs Scrunch AI
**Their strength:** Active content production
**Their weakness:** Narrow focus, limited engine coverage
**AnswerMonk's wedge:** "Four engines, not one. Complete visibility."

### Vs "doing nothing" (the real competitor)
Most buyers aren't choosing between AnswerMonk and Profound. They're choosing between "buy an AEO tool" and "keep ignoring AI visibility for now."

**Wedge:** Make "doing nothing" expensive. Show them their competitors are already being cited. Data from AnswerMonk's product + the flagship study does this.

## Positioning drift red flags

If anyone on the team ever uses any of these phrases, correct immediately:

- "AnswerMonk is like Semrush for AI" → No, Semrush is for search engines; we're for AI engines (different retrieval mechanism)
- "It's an AI SEO tool" → No, SEO is rankings; we're citations (different outcome)
- "We use AI to..." → No, we TRACK AI, we don't USE AI (confusing positioning)
- "We're an AI tool" → Too broad; we're specifically an AI VISIBILITY tool
- "We help with AEO/GEO/LLMO" → Specific is better: "we track and improve citations in AI engines"
