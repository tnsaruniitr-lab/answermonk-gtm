## Anti-patterns (explicitly avoid)

1. ❌ Publishing unedited AI-generated content (Google's Helpful Content System flags)
2. ❌ Hit-piece competitor reviews (Profound's `/scrunch-ai-review` got deindexed — ours would too)
3. ❌ Targeting "aeo" raw keyword (American Eagle Outfitters contamination — 32K volume is apparel)
4. ❌ Listicles without first-party testing methodology
5. ❌ Free tier without limits (optimizes product for non-payers per Sieve [PB:437])
6. ❌ Press releases without evergreen twin pages (70% link equity evaporates in 90 days)
7. ❌ Broad "B2B brands" positioning without ICP
8. ❌ Delaying founder LinkedIn (cuts 40% off distribution effectiveness)
9. ❌ Launching all product silos at once (narrow wedge required per Sieve [P:2064, R:2744])
10. ❌ Chasing KD 55+ keywords before DR 30 (18-month ROI, wastes capacity)

## Strategic principle that governs everything

**"AnswerMonk is the only company in this category whose product directly measures its own marketing KPI."**

Every content piece, tool, and study should be powered by AnswerMonk's own citation tracking data. This is the unfair advantage competitors can't cross without building AnswerMonk's product.

The flywheel:
```
Product measures citations
  → citations become study data
  → study drives links/authority
  → authority drives rankings
  → rankings drive signups
  → more users = more product data
  → bigger next study
```

Each turn compounds. Build this flywheel, not scattered content.
