# 00 — Strategic Overview

**Purpose:** The strategic foundation. What AnswerMonk is building, why, and the 10 ranked actions that define the first 90 days.

---

## The thesis in one paragraph

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is a new category with no established category leader. Profound has the most funding ($96M Series C) and organic traffic (26.9K/mo) but their moat is concentrated in 1-2 pieces of content. The entire category is ~18 months old and the terminology itself ("AEO vs GEO vs LLMO") isn't settled. AnswerMonk can achieve category leadership with ~15 canonical pages + 3 free tools + 1 flagship study + founder brand — NOT with 150 blog posts or a massive SEO play. **Category leadership in an emerging market is a concentration problem, not a scale problem.**

## The 3 Unfair Advantages

### Advantage 1: The Product-Data Flywheel
AnswerMonk is the only vendor whose product directly measures the KPI it needs to optimize. Every other AEO company infers citation data; AnswerMonk has it.

**Exploit ruthlessly:** Every study, benchmark, dashboard (AMQ Score, AVI, Feature Matrix, Citation Gap Study) powered by AnswerMonk's own aggregate data. This is a moat competitors can't cross without building your product.

**The flywheel:**
```
Product measures citations
  → citations become study data
  → study drives links/authority
  → links drive rankings
  → rankings drive signups
  → more users = more product data
  → bigger next study
```

### Advantage 2: Category-Naming Rights
The AEO/GEO/LLMO terminology is unsettled. First vendor to define terms (AMQ Score, AVI, 5-Layer GEO Stack) forces competitors to reference your vocabulary. Profound hasn't taken this step because they waited too long; you can still act.

**Window:** Before Q3 2026 (category will harden around whatever names are canonical by then).

### Advantage 3: Founder-as-Measurement-Expert Positioning
The space has vendors but no category voice:
- Profound: no breakout founder persona
- Otterly: product-led
- Peec: enterprise-sales-led

If AnswerMonk's founder publishes weekly (AVI updates, study teasers, benchmark drops) with authoritative technical tone — not marketing posts — they can occupy the Lenny-Rachitsky / Amanda-Natividad slot for AI visibility within 12 months.

## 5 Common Mistakes to Avoid

(Observed directly in competitor data)

### Mistake 1: AI-flagged content without human signals
Profound's `/blog/ai-search-volatility` is ranked "Very High" on AI Content Level. Google dropped it 5 positions.

**Avoid:** Shipping AI-drafted content without first-party bylines, LinkedIn sameAs, original screenshots, first-party data, methodology sections. See `09-blog-rules.md` for the 7 required human signals.

### Mistake 2: Hit-piece reviews
Profound's `/blog/scrunch-ai-review` got deindexed entirely. Writesonic review stuck at low traffic.

**Avoid:** Hit-piece framing. Use balanced 5-pros/5-cons template for all competitor reviews.

### Mistake 3: Press-release dependency without evergreen twins
Otterly's G2 award post, Profound's product launch announcement, and Series A page all generated initial link spikes and then decayed within 90 days.

**Avoid:** Every time-bound announcement must point into a permanent aggregator page (/awards, /news, /product-updates).

### Mistake 4: Commercial funnel before authority funnel
Attempting to rank for "best ai seo software" (KD 41) or "generative engine optimization" (KD 75) at DR 0 is wasted effort. G2/Capterra dominate.

**Avoid:** Target commercial high-KD terms until DR 30+.

### Mistake 5: Free tier without limits
Sieve [PB:437] — free tiers optimize product for non-payers.

**Avoid:** Permanent free tier with full product access. Use free TOOLS (single-check, email-gated) + paid PRODUCT (subscription).

## Operating Non-Negotiables

Three principles that must be internalized:

### Non-negotiable 1: The 200-query benchmark is the KPI
If team members present "we rank for 300 keywords" as a win, pull back to "how many of the 200 canonical queries cite us." Rankings are a leading indicator; LLM citations are the lagging truth.

### Non-negotiable 2: One canonical page per week, no more
Shipping 3 shallow pages costs more in LLM citation probability than shipping 1 reference-grade page. Quality over volume is mathematical in an LLM-first world — the 15-page strategy only works if each page meets the structural bar.

Exception: Weeks 1-2 can ship 2/week because glossary entries share a template and are short.

### Non-negotiable 3: Founder LinkedIn ≥ 3 posts/week
The distribution bottleneck for B2B SaaS in 2026 is founder voice. If the founder disappears, content you ship vanishes into the void. Sieve [P:1341] validates LinkedIn 113% ROAS attributed to founder brand.
